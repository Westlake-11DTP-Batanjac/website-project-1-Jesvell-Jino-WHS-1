The Alpaca is a smaller camelid that is also domesticated in the Andes. It is best known for its soft, warm wool, which is used to make clothing. Alpacas are usually kept in herds and are well adapted to cold mountain climates.

The Guanaco is a wild camelid that lives in the mountains and open plains of South America. It is fast and alert, which helps it escape predators. Guanacos are well suited to harsh environments and can survive in cold, dry regions.

The Llama is a strong, domesticated camelid from South America. It lives mainly in the Andes Mountains and is often used by people to carry heavy loads across rough terrain. Llamas are hardy animals that can survive in cold, high-altitude environments.
